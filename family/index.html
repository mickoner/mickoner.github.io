Family

Placeholder.

We’re like any family. All fucked up. And beautiful. Most of it doesn’t need to be documented, recorded for posterity. But this one does. This was supposed to be a project that I worked on with my mom. But she died before we got around to it. So I do this as a unfinished business, an homage, a story she wanted told. The subject of this exercise, my cousin, will, I suspect, call me “obsessed” with him. I’m not. I think of it more from my failed, dispassionate journalism student side.
